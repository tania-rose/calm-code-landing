The Why

Have you ever been in a room where someone said, let’s all slow down and take a breath?

Isn’t it curious how they are always right? It always helps.

ARM is the training that makes you that person.

The Method

For the moments no one trains you for.

ARM is a seven-day training in becoming the person who can actually help when someone’s nervous system goes sideways. Yours included.

You’ve been in the room. Someone is panicking. The air tightens. People look at the floor. You want to help. You don’t know how.

That is the room ARM is built for.

How it works

Anchor. Attune. Apply.

A

Anchor

Regulate yourself first.

Before you can help anyone, you have to get there yourself. ARM starts with your breath, not theirs. The body across from you is reading your nervous system before it hears a word you say. Settle yours, and you’ve already done half the work.

A

Attune

Track the felt sense.

You learn to read what is actually happening. Activation. Settling. Where someone is, and what they can handle. Most of this work happens before anyone takes a deeper breath.

A

Apply

Deliver one of two protocols.

When the moment lands, you reach for one of two tools. One takes 90 seconds. One takes 12 minutes. Both work in any room, with any nervous system. We don’t teach fifty techniques. We teach two you’ll have for the rest of your life.

What you walk away with

  • A way of breathing most people never access in their lifetime.
  • Two protocols you can deliver from memory, anywhere.
  • A working map of the nervous system that respects your scope and stretches your range.
  • The one capability that changes everything. The ability to be the regulated body in the room.

The Journey

Six movements. One transformation.

Over seven days at ASHA, something specific shifts inside you each day. Not a curriculum to memorise. A path the body walks.

01

Day one

Remember

You come back to your own breath. Before any technique, you sit with what has always been there. The training begins by remembering, not by learning.

02

Day two

Reframe

You stop thinking of breath as relaxation. You start understanding it as something that changes your state, your body, and the room you walk into.

03

Day three

Resource

The felt sense becomes a tool you can draw on. You stop being lost when things get charged. You have something to come back to, every time.

04

Day four

Regulate

You learn the 90-second protocol. You can settle yourself on demand. A tool that wasn’t yours before this week, and will be yours for every week after.

05

Day five

Realign

The three-dimensional breath. The 12-minute protocol. You start using breath for the room, not just for yourself. Your nervous system becomes useful to others.

06

Days six and seven

Rise

You deliver your own session in your own voice. You walk out as the person who can do this when it matters. The certificate is just the paper. The real graduation is the body you leave with.

The Working Map

You don’t need to be an expert. You need enough to know what you’re holding.

Four concepts. Enough literacy to feel grounded in any room. Never so much it crosses into clinical.

Window of tolerance

The band where thinking, connecting, and learning are possible.

Pendulation

Moving consciously between activation and settling. Not forcing calm.

Titration

Working with intensity in small doses. Not flooding, not suppressing.

Co-regulation

When you’re regulated, the person across from you has somewhere to go.

The training stays inside the line it draws.

A note on scope

ARM is a regulation tool. It isn’t therapy, and it doesn’t teach clinical diagnosis or treatment. The training stays inside that line and gives you a clear sense of when to slow down, when to ground, and when to refer.

For practitioners who want to go deeper into facilitation, ARM is the foundation of the wider Alchemy of Breath pathway. Graduates can continue from there.
