The Methodology

Your nervous system is trainable.
This is the architecture you’ll teach.

For 14 years, we’ve been teaching people to consciously breathe. Now we’re giving it the structure it deserves. Six stages of regulation, received in your own body across the week. The same six stages you’ll guide others through as a certified Coach. Tap any stage to see what it builds.

Stage One

Remember

You come back to your own breath. Before any technique, before any protocol, you sit with what has always been there.

This is what Anthony calls marrying your breath. A return to what the body already knew. Quieter than you remembered. More available than you knew.

Stage Two

Reframe

You stop thinking of breath as relaxation or deep breathing. You start understanding it as a three-dimensional, nervous-system intervention.

The body work lands here, and so does the history. What you’ve been carrying becomes visible, and there’s room to set some of it down.

Stage Three

Resource

The felt sense becomes something you can draw on. Window of tolerance, pendulation, titration, co-regulation. They stop being terminology and start being how you read what’s happening inside you.

You stop being lost when things get charged. You always have somewhere to come back to.

Stage Four

Regulate

The 90-second protocol arrives. You can settle yourself on demand. In a meeting, in traffic, in the middle of the thing you used to lose yourself inside of.

A tool you didn’t have seven days ago. One that will be yours for every week after.

Stage Five

Realign

The three-dimensional breath and the 12-minute protocol arrive together. Two practices that meet the nervous system where it actually lives.

You stop using breath only for yourself. You start using it for others. Your calm nervous system becomes someone else’s anchor. A signal of safety.

Stage Six · The summit

Rise

The practicum. You deliver your own session, in your own voice, with your own hands.

You graduate as the person who can do this when it matters. For yourself first. And if you choose, for the people you serve.

After You Leave
