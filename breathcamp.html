What BreathCamp Is

A week of breath,
body, and community.

BreathCamp is a 7-day, 6-night residential retreat that combines conscious breathing with movement, sound, and the kind of deep connection that only happens when you live and practice alongside others for a full week.

It is led by Anthony Abbagnano, Amy Rachelle, and a team of senior Alchemy of Breath trainers, on 37 acres of quiet Tuscan land. Three meals a day. Plant-based, locally sourced. Practice spaces under the open sky when the weather is kind.

The week is designed for people who want to reset their nervous system, find their community, and spend time in one of the most beautiful places in Italy. No prior breathwork experience required.

A Needed Pause

When the world feels loud,
stillness becomes a form of care.

The last few years have asked a lot of us. Wars that do not end, headlines that do not soften, a climate that keeps reminding us it is listening, and a pace of life that treats rest like a luxury instead of a requirement. If your nervous system feels tired, it is not a personal failing. It is a reasonable response to the moment we are living in.

A week in Tuscany will not fix what is happening in the world, and we will not pretend otherwise. What it can offer is a quiet room to set things down for a while. Slow mornings, honest breath, food grown nearby, and the company of people who also came looking for ground beneath their feet. Not an escape from your life, but a place to remember how to meet it.

About ASHA

Welcome Home.

ASHA Retreat Centre sits on 37 acres of untouched Tuscan land near Castell'Azzara. Founded in 2020 by Anthony Abbagnano and Amy Rachelle, it was built as a sanctuary for healing, creativity, and community. A place where all of you is welcome.

Swimming pool, meditation spaces, dining room, group practice rooms including the open-air Prana Shala. Nearby natural hot springs, Etruscan historical sites, and Pitigliano if you want to wander.
