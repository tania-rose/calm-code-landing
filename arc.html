What is ARC

A residency for those drawn to slowness.

ARC is co-living for people who want more than a one-week retreat. Stay a few nights or a few months. Write, recover, breathe, garden, walk the hills. You set the pace.

Not a hotel. Not a retreat. Something quieter.

ARC residents are working remotely, finishing manuscripts, healing, transitioning, or just remembering how to rest. You have your own private room and access to the full ASHA campus — meditation halls, breathing rooms, woodland trails, and a community kitchen serving locally-grown vegetarian food.

You can join breathwork sessions, sit out in the garden, or do absolutely nothing. The land tends to take over.

A day at ARC

The land sets the tempo.

Nothing is mandatory. Everything is available. This is a sample, not a schedule.

7:00

First light over the hills

Tea in the kitchen. Sit with the dogs. Walk to the breathing temple if you feel pulled.

8:30

Breath circle (optional)

30-minute conscious breath practice with whoever is on the land that morning.

10:00

Breakfast under the pergola

Fresh fruit, bread, eggs from the chickens. Long, unhurried, with the morning still cool.

11:00 — 14:00

Your own work

Wifi in the library, sun on the stone bench, garden tasks, writing, or sleep. Yours.

14:30

Lunch at the long table

The big meal of the day. Mostly garden, often with retreatants and staff.

17:00

River, sauna or walk

Cool water, hot wood, or footpaths. The Tuscan summer is long and forgiving.

21:00

Night sky & soft conversation

Fire, stars, music, silence. Whichever the night calls for.

Common questions

Things people ask first.

Do I have to join the breathwork sessions?

No. ARC is co-living, not a programme. Sessions are open to residents who feel called. Many residents come for the rhythm of the land and never set foot in the breathing temple. Both are welcomed.

How do I get there?

Closest airports are Rome Fiumicino, Pisa, and Florence. We send a full transport guide once you've reserved, including shared shuttle days from Grosseto train station.

Is there reliable wifi for remote work?

Yes — fibre wifi in the main house, library, and most outdoor seating areas. Plenty of residents work full-time from here.

Can I bring my partner / friend / dog?

Partners and friends are welcome — just let us know. We don't currently host pets, with rare exceptions for assistance animals.

What's the food like?

Three meals a day, mostly vegan and vegetarian, mostly grown on the land. We accommodate gluten-free and most allergies. The food is one of the things people remember most.

What's the minimum stay?

Three nights. Most residents stay one to four weeks. Long stays of one to three months are possible at reduced monthly rates — ask in the form.
