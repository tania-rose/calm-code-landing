# Voice guide

Every line of copy on every Alchemy of Breath page follows this guide. No exceptions.

## The tone in one line

Grounded, honest, spacious. A wise friend who has done the work, not a coach selling an outcome.

## Hard rules

### 1. No em dashes. Ever.
Replace with periods, commas, "and", or restructure.

- Bad: "The breath is a tool — simple, powerful, and always with you."
- Good: "The breath is a tool. Simple, powerful, and always with you."

If you see `—`, `–`, or ` - ` used as an em dash in existing copy, fix it.

### 2. American spelling
- colour → color
- organisation → organization
- realise → realize
- centre → center
- favourite → favorite
- programme → program
- behaviour → behavior
- analyse → analyze

**Exception:** "ASHA Retreat Centre" is a proper noun. Keep the British spelling.

### 3. No overpromising
Never claim outcomes you cannot guarantee. Nobody transforms in a week. Nobody unlocks anything.

- Bad: "Transform your life in 7 days."
- Good: "Seven days to sit with your breath, your body, and the people you came with."

### 4. No superlatives
Avoid entirely: "life-changing", "transformative", "breakthrough", "unlock", "revolutionary", "secret", "ultimate", "guaranteed", "proven", "science-backed" (unless actually citing science).

"World-class" appears once on BreathCamp page in a specific context about the venue. Do not propagate it.

### 5. No fake urgency
- Allowed: "A few spots left" (if true), "September cohort closes October 15" (if true).
- Not allowed: Countdown timers that reset. "Last chance" when it isn't. "Only today" when it isn't.

### 6. No therapy claims
Every page footer includes a disclaimer: "This is an educational program. Not a substitute for medical or psychological treatment."

## Vocabulary

### Preferred terms
- **Graduate** (not student, alumnus, or participant after completion)
- **Facilitator** (not coach, instructor, therapist, or healer)
- **The work** (not the journey, the process, the healing)
- **Hold space** (not guide, lead, or heal)
- **Practice** (not protocol, system, or technique when talking about personal use)
- **Session** (not class, when 1:1 or small group)
- **Training** (for the certification programs)
- **Retreat / immersion** (for residential)
- **Cohort** (for a specific group going through together)
- **Meet yourself** / **sit with** / **notice** (for inner work)

### Banned terms
- transform / transformation
- breakthrough
- unlock
- revolutionary
- secret
- ultimate
- guaranteed
- proven (unless citing a specific study)
- science-backed (same)
- healing journey (pick one: "the work" or "your practice")
- step into your power
- manifest
- next-level
- game-changer
- hack (as in "breathing hack")
- biohacking
- master (as a verb: "master your breath" — too aggressive)

### Words we use differently from most wellness brands
- **"Meet"** instead of "discover" or "find"
- **"Sit with"** instead of "process" or "work through"
- **"Notice"** instead of "become aware of" or "realize"
- **"Held"** instead of "supported"

## Sentence patterns that work

### The honest-framing pattern
State what you will NOT promise, then state what you CAN offer.

> "We will not promise you a new life. We will sit with you while you notice the one you are already living, and what might be ready to change."

> "A week in Tuscany will not fix what is happening in the world. What it can offer is a quiet room to set things down for a while."

### The "who this is not for" pattern
Explicit disqualification builds trust.

> "This might not be the right fit if you are looking for a weekend or fast-track certification."

### The "it is not X" disclaimer
Clarify what the program isn't, before anyone has to ask.

> "It is not therapy. It is not a bootcamp. It is a quiet, structured way of meeting yourself on purpose."

### The lived-experience voice
Speak from experience, not from a pedestal.

> "Every facilitator I have trained started with the same doubt you might be feeling right now."

### The pace/rhythm sentence
Short sentences. Space between ideas. Resist packing.

- Bad: "Our 400-hour program includes weekly live mentorship calls with experienced facilitators and a 7-day immersion at our Tuscan retreat center."
- Good: "400 hours of training. Weekly live mentorship. A 7-day immersion at ASHA in Tuscany."

## Sentence length

- Aim for variable rhythm. Mix 4-word and 20-word sentences.
- Over 30 words almost always needs a split.
- Never write a paragraph that is one sentence of 60 words.

## How to replace em dashes mechanically

| Original (with em dash) | Rewrite |
|---|---|
| "X — Y" (parenthetical) | "X. Y." or "X, Y." |
| "X — Y." (emphasis) | "X. Y." |
| "A, B — and C." | "A, B, and C." |
| "We do X — not Y." | "We do X. Not Y." |
| "Y — simple, clear." | "Y. Simple, clear." |

## Copy-check pass

Before marking a page done, search the file for:
- `—` (em dash)
- `–` (en dash used as em dash)
- " - " (spaced hyphen used as em dash)
- `colour`, `organisation`, `realise`, `favour`, `programme`, `behaviour`, `analyse`
- `transform`, `breakthrough`, `unlock`, `revolutionary`, `guaranteed`, `proven`
- `master your`, `hack your`, `next-level`, `step into your power`

Fix each one.

## The test

Would Anthony say this out loud, to someone he cared about, over coffee?

If not, rewrite it.
