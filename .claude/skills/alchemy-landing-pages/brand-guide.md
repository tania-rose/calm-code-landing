# Brand guide

## Positioning

Alchemy of Breath is an educational organization teaching breathwork as a tool for life. It is not a clinic, not a church, not a brand of wellness. It is a school.

The unique thing is the combination: grounded respiratory science, contemplative inner work, a home (ASHA in Tuscany), and a 12+ year body of practice. Most breathwork schools have one or two of these. Few have all.

## What AoB is and is not

### Is
- An educational school with a training path
- A residential sanctuary (ASHA)
- A community of graduates working in 40+ countries
- A place for practitioners who want to add breath to their work
- Anthony Abbagnano's life work, grounded in practice rather than theory

### Is not
- A therapy practice
- A medical service
- A clinic
- A belief system
- A weekend certification mill
- A psychedelic-adjacent space (even though conscious connected breathwork can produce non-ordinary states)

## The four offerings (tiers)

Pages build on each other. A graduate of tier 1 is a good candidate for tier 2, and so on. Cross-sell between them via the `cross-sell.html` component.

### Level 1: Alchemy Regulation Method (formerly "The Calm Code")
- **Format**: 7-day residential in Tuscany + 10 hours online pre-study
- **Audience**: Practitioners (coaches, therapists, yoga teachers, healthcare pros) who want to add breath to their work without full facilitator certification
- **Core promise**: Confidence with breath as a regulation tool, for yourself first, then for the people you work with
- **Price range**: €1,701 – €2,738 depending on accommodation
- **Primary CTA**: Book Your Place at ASHA
- **Template**: `templates/residential.html`

### Level 2: Inner Journey
- **Format**: 7-chapter video course, self-paced or guided (14-week live cohort)
- **Audience**: Anyone ready to do inner work. No prior breathwork needed.
- **Core promise**: A structured container to meet the patterns running your life
- **Price**: $499 self-paced / $2,297 guided mastery
- **Primary CTA**: Start the Journey
- **Template**: `templates/online-course.html`
- **Note**: This is the north-star design for the whole site.

### Level 3: Facilitator Training
- **Format**: 8-month online (400 hours) + BreathCamp graduation
- **Audience**: People called to hold space for others. Some prior breathwork experience helpful but not required.
- **Core promise**: Certified facilitation with real business infrastructure after
- **Price**: $6,700 (pay in full, super early bird) up to $8,064 (8-month plan)
- **Primary CTA**: Apply Now
- **Template**: `templates/certification.html`

### Level 4: BreathCamp
- **Format**: 7-day residential retreat at ASHA, 5 sessions per year
- **Audience**: Anyone wanting a deep breathwork week. No prior experience needed.
- **Core promise**: A week of breath, community, and Tuscan sun
- **Price**: From €1,319 (early bird, bring your own tent) to €6,895 (private cottage)
- **Primary CTA**: Choose Your Dates
- **Template**: `templates/residential.html`

## CTA hierarchy

Every page uses this three-tier structure.

### Primary: commit
Gold button. The main conversion action.

Copy variants by tier:
- Tier 1 (Regulation Method): "Book Your Place at ASHA" / "Secure Your Place"
- Tier 2 (Inner Journey): "Start the Journey" / "Choose Your Path"
- Tier 3 (Facilitator Training): "Apply Now"
- Tier 4 (BreathCamp): "Choose Your Dates" / "Book This Session"

### Secondary: try before you commit
Outline button. For people not ready to commit today.

**Default: "Try a Free Breathwork Session"** routing to free resources (meditation library, intro class, sample chapter, or whatever the on-ramp is).

Use sparingly: hero and final CTA. Not every card needs it.

### Tertiary: talk to us
Text link or small outline button. Lowest-friction option.

Copy: "Book a Discovery Call" routing to the Calendly / GHL discovery call page.

Appears in:
- FAQ section closer ("Still have questions? Book a free 15-minute call.")
- Footer
- Final CTA as the secondary option if "Try a Free Breathwork Session" doesn't fit the context

## Founder voice: Anthony Abbagnano

Anthony is the narrator behind most first-person copy. His voice:
- Direct but warm
- Speaks from personal practice, not authority
- Uses "you" sparingly. Prefers "we" or "I" or impersonal.
- Does not moralize. Describes.
- Will tell you what the program isn't before telling you what it is.

Example lines in his voice:
> "I built this training because I saw what breathwork did for people who had tried everything else."
> "Every facilitator I have trained started with the same doubt you might be feeling right now."
> "We will not promise you a new life. We will sit with you while you notice the one you are already living."

## Disclaimers (required in every footer)

Verbatim, or close to it:

> "[Program name] is an educational program. It is not a substitute for medical or psychological treatment. If you have a mental health condition, please consult your doctor or therapist before beginning."

For facilitator training add:
> "Breathwork facilitation is not a substitute for medical or psychological care. Results vary by individual."

## Photography guidance

### What works
- Real AoB sessions at ASHA (the canonical Tuscany set)
- Instructor portraits in natural light with slight grayscale
- Landscape / grounds / olive groves
- Close-ups of hands, breath, still objects

### What doesn't
- Stock photos of people meditating on beaches
- AI-generated imagery
- Overly posed group shots with identical smiles
- Screenshots of Zoom calls (use real session photos instead)

## Music / sound
Not on landing pages. Silence is the default. If a video plays, it is muted until the user clicks.

## The test, again
If a line of copy or a design decision feels like it belongs on a typical wellness coaching site, it probably doesn't belong here. Alchemy is quieter, more grounded, and more honest than the category average. Hold that standard.
